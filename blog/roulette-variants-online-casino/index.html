The Physics of the Wheel: Single vs. Double Zero

The primary distinction between the two variants lies in the physical configuration of the wheel. European roulette features 37 pockets: numbers 1 through 36 and a single green zero (0). Conversely, the American version introduces a second green pocket—the double zero (00)—bringing the total to 38 pockets. While that single extra pocket might seem insignificant, it is the primary engine behind the variance in house edge. Because the payout for a single number remains 35:1 in both versions, the addition of the 00 in American roulette significantly diminishes the player's statistical probability of winning.

Comparing House Edge and Winning Odds

The house edge is the mathematical advantage the casino holds over the player over the long term. In European roulette, the presence of only one zero results in a house edge of 2.70%. In the American variation, the second zero increases that edge to 5.26%. Table Layouts and Betting Options

The betting table layout mirrors the wheel. On an American table, you will see the additional 00 section integrated into the betting grid. While this allows for unique bets like the "Five-Number Bet" (covering 0, 00, 1, 2, and 3), this specific wager carries an even higher house edge of approximately 7.89%, making it one of the most unfavorable bets in the entire casino. Most European tables are cleaner and prioritize "inside" and "outside" bets that offer a more consistent experience for those who enjoy playing at established sites like those highlighted in our Casumo casino review.

Outside Bets

These cover larger sections of the board, such as Red/Black, Even/Odd, or High/Low. They offer higher winning probabilities but lower payouts, making them ideal for steady play.

Inside Bets

These target specific numbers or small groups of numbers. While they provide the thrill of high 35:1 payouts, they carry a significantly higher variance and lower hit frequency.
