What is RTP and How Does It Work?

The Return to Player (RTP) percentage is a theoretical figure that indicates how much of the total money wagered on a specific slot machine is paid back to players over the long term. For instance, if a game features an RTP of 96.50%, the game is designed to return an average of £96.50 for every £100 wagered over millions of spins. It is important to remember that this is a mathematical average, not a guaranteed return for every individual session.

RTP is the inverse of the "house edge." If a game has a 96% RTP, the house edge is 4%. By choosing games with higher RTP percentages, you are essentially reducing the house's statistical advantage. Understanding Volatility: High vs. Low

While RTP tells you about the potential payout over the long term, volatility (or variance) tells you about the nature of those wins during your current session. Think of volatility as the "risk-reward" profile of a game:

  • Low Volatility: These slots offer frequent, smaller wins. They are ideal for players who want to keep their bankroll stable and enjoy longer gameplay sessions.
  • Medium Volatility: A balance between frequent small wins and the occasional larger payout.
  • High Volatility: These games pay out less frequently, but when they do, the wins can be substantial. These are favored by players seeking the "big win" potential, even if it comes with higher risk to the bankroll.
Key takeaway: RTP determines the long-term mathematical return, while volatility defines how those returns are distributed—either as consistent, small frequent payouts or erratic, high-value jackpots.
